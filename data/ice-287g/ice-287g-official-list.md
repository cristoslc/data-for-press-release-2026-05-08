# ICE 287(g) Program — Official Information

## Current Statistics (as of May 5, 2026)

- **1,786 Memorandums of Agreement** signed
- Covers **39 states and 2 U.S. Territories**
- **JEM** (Jail Enforcement Model): 178 agencies in 30 states + 1 territory
- **WSO** (Warrant Service Officer): 501 agencies in 35 states
- **TFM** (Task Force Model): 1,107 agencies in 32 states + 2 territories

## Program Description

Section 287(g) of the Immigration and Nationality Act (added by the Illegal Immigration Reform and Immigrant Responsibility Act of 1996) authorizes ICE to delegate state and local law enforcement officers the authority to perform specified immigration officer functions under ICE's direction and oversight.

## Executive Order Context

On January 20, 2025, President Trump issued Executive Order 14159, "Protecting the American People Against Invasion," which requires ICE to authorize state and local law enforcement officials under 287(g) "to the maximum extent permitted by law."

## States with Mandatory Legislation

- **Florida** and **Georgia** have passed state legislation mandating law enforcement agencies seek out or enter into 287(g) MOAs with ICE.

## Three Program Models

1. **Jail Enforcement Model (JEM):** Identify and process removable aliens with criminal charges arrested by state/local LEAs.
2. **Task Force Model (TFM):** Force multiplier — limited immigration authority during routine police duties.
3. **Warrant Service Officer (WSO):** Train, certify, and authorize officers to serve administrative warrants on aliens in custody.

### Cross-Reference with SPPD Shared Networks (2026-05-07)

The parsed ICE official list was cross-referenced against the 724 agencies in SPPD's Flock shared network spreadsheet (`Shared Networks + 287g.xlsx`). Results:

- **118 agencies** were originally marked "Yes" for 287(g) participation. **All 118 are verified on the ICE official list.**
- **26 additional agencies** were found on the ICE official list that the spreadsheet marked "No" (confirmed misses — same county/city in same state).
- **9 probable additional misses** were identified (strong same-state name match, needs manual confirmation).
- **Revised estimate: 144 confirmed 287(g) participants** (19.9% of shared network), up to **153 with probable** (21.1%).

The original spreadsheet undercounted 287(g) participants by 22-30%.
