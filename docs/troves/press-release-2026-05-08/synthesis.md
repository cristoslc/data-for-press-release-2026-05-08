# Synthesis — Press Release May 8, 2026

## Source

**no-flock-for-sopo-press-release** — Official press release from No Flock for SoPo (May 8, 2026).

## Key findings

1. **602+ agencies** in 44 states have been granted access to South Portland's Flock Safety cameras, including **more than 70 with active ICE collaboration agreements** (287(g) or similar).
2. **SPPD's own Flock audit logs** show:
   - Over **2,000 searches** (2,007 raw audit log rows; 1,572 unique search sessions after deduplication — the "over 2,000" claim counts raw per-network query rows) since April 1, 2025.
   - **107 instances** (more than 100) of searches conducted on behalf of or through coordination with other agencies (67 explicitly cross-agency; 40 through statewide systems such as MIAC).
   - Nearly **two-thirds** (67 of 107 = 62.6%) of those cross-agency searches were for unnamed/unidentified law enforcement organizations.
3. The **Florida Fish and Wildlife Conservation Commission** — which has searched Flock data for ICE — is among the agencies with SPPD camera access.
4. SPPD-Flock email correspondence shows **confusion** about how South Portland's data was being shared/protected. *(Not supported by quantitative data in this repo — outside scope.)*
5. Flock cameras have been used to track people seeking **abortion care**, monitor **No Kings protesters**, and perform lookups for **ICE**.
6. **Denver** and **Cambridge, MA** have ended Flock programs over privacy concerns.
7. South Portland City Council will decide on Flock's future during a **budget workshop on May 12, 2026** at 6:30 PM.
8. This follows the Portland Press Herald's reporting on **Chief Dan Ahern's texts with ICE agents** during "Operation Catch of the Day," including Ahern congratulating agents — despite South Portland's explicit resolution against ICE collaboration.
