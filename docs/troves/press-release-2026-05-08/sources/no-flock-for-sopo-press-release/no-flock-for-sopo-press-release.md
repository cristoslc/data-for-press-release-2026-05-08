# BREAKING: New Records Reveal Nationwide Police Access to Mainers' Daily Lives

## Over 600 agencies around the country, including more than 70 with a public commitment to collaborating with ICE, have access to South Portland's AI-powered Flock surveillance cameras

**South Portland, Maine — May 8, 2026** — South Portland Police records reveal that Mainers' daily driving patterns have been exposed to Flock Safety's searchable nationwide database for months. Flock's technology hosts an unregulated network for warrantless searches all over America. No Flock for SoPo is calling for an immediate end to South Portland's Flock contract.

Flock cameras, a type of Automatic License Plate Reader (ALPRs), capture distinguishing features of every vehicle that drives by. Flock cameras have been used to conduct lookups for ICE, search for people seeking abortion care, and track participants in No Kings protests. Cities including Denver, Colorado, and Cambridge, Massachusetts, have recently ended their Flock programs due to resident concerns.

Records released this week via FOAA requests by No Flock for SoPo indicate that more than 70 law enforcement organizations with active ICE collaboration agreements are among the over 600 agencies that have been granted access to South Portland's cameras. South Portland has shared Flock network access with groups in 44 states, including the Florida Fish and Wildlife Commission, which has searched Flock data on behalf of ICE. Email correspondence between SPPD and Flock even highlights confusion about how South Portland's data is shared.

"Everyone who's moved through South Portland—residents, neighbors, and visitors alike—deserves to know that they're being monitored. It isn't a benign effort to solve dangerous crimes. It's a nationwide surveillance dragnet with terrifying ramifications for civil rights, bodily autonomy, and First Amendment Freedoms," said Willys DeVoll from No Flock for SoPo.

South Portland's own Flock data reveal that SPPD has been sharing information: Logs reveal over 2000 searches by SPPD since April 1, 2025, and more than 100 instances of officers performing searches on behalf of other agencies. Nearly two-thirds of those were conducted on behalf of unnamed or unidentified law enforcement organizations. This comes just weeks after the Portland Press-Herald published previously secret texts from SPPD Chief Dan Ahern revealing a similar pattern of informal communication with ICE, including Ahern congratulating ICE agents during this winter's Operation Catch of the Day. South Portland has an explicit resolution against collaboration with federal immigration enforcement beyond what is required by law.

South Portland City Council will decide on the future of Flock during their budget workshop at 6:30pm on Tuesday, May 12, at South Portland City Hall. No Flock for SoPo invites concerned residents to join us in demanding that the City immediately end its contract with Flock.

## About No Flock for SoPo

No Flock for SoPo is a resident group committed to abolishing the use of AI-powered surveillance by the South Portland Police Department, including Automated License Plate Readers (ALPRs). Promoting a safe community for all begins with ending the use of AI-networked and cloud-based surveillance technology in South Portland.
